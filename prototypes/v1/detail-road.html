Description

A father and his son walk alone through burned America. Nothing moves in the ravaged landscape save the ash on the wind. It is cold enough to crack stones, and when the snow falls it is gray.

The sky is dark and the days are short and cold. The journey south takes them through a bleak landscape where gangs of marauders scavenge what little remains. The boy's father carries a pistol to use as a last resort. They are the good guys, carrying the fire.

Review
One of those books where the prose style and the subject matter are so locked together that you can't imagine the story told any other way. McCarthy strips out punctuation, strips out most of the world, and what's left is just two people moving through it. The boy is the thing. The father explains himself through his relationship to the boy better than any interior monologue could.
